Karen Martin-Bond

"Give me problems, give me work." - Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

Consultant, facilitator, trainer, and coach.

I specialise in strengthening work relationships and breaking down silos in organisations. I work with teams and individuals to explore and enhance their leadership, teamwork, communication and culture change.

I design (and deliver) multi-layered training programmes that can be made up of forums, action learning groups, workshops, one-to-one sessions and online content (i.e. e-learning).

ACHIEVEMENTS

Over my illustrious career, I have:

  • Designed and facilitated both small and large leadership forums.
  • Co-designed and/or facilitated organisation-wide leadership, culture change and communication programmes.
  • Designed and delivered numerous action learning groups, ‘train the trainer’ programmes, skill-based workshops and team workshops.
  • Coached a number of people for a variety of professional development purposes.
  • In collaboration with learning solution companies, designed and delivered e-learning modules.
  • I have also had the privilege of being a senior adviser both in an L&D team at DIA and an OD team at MBIE, during two separate years.

SPECIALISATION

  • The Five Dysfunctions of a Team model and process; designed to move a team from dysfunction or discord to a positive level of self-awareness, behaviour and capability.
  • The Team Management Profile; with teams and their leaders, as well as with individuals exploring how to make best use of their work preferences.
  • Conflict resolution for individuals and teams; using various “games”, negotiation techniques and managed conversations, I can assist people to uncover the real problems and find solutions that work.
  • Well-designed and respected 360° feedback profiles on leadership, emotional & social intelligence and strategic intelligence.
  • The GROW model for successful coaching, both in skill and behavioural/developmental growth.
  • The public sector’s Leadership Success Profile, for both performance improvement and development.
  • Life transitions: both in career and towards retirement. Both programmes involve imparting thorough research and “best practice” as well as the encouragement of personal reflection.
